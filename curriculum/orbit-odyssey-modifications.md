# BearBots — Orbit Odyssey Game Modifications

*This document captures all differences between standard Orbit Odyssey rules
and the BearBots summer camp version. Reference this before writing student
materials, lesson content, or competition day procedures.*

---

## Overview

BearBots runs Orbit Odyssey in a **1v1 round-robin format** with **identical
robot chassis** for all students. Standard rules apply except where noted below.
The field, field elements, and match structure are unchanged.

---

## Game Elements

| Element | Quantity | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rubble | 12 | Pre-loaded on field | Standard from official game |
| Ping pong balls | TBD | Side hoppers, player-loaded | BearBots addition |
| Large ball | 1 | Pre-placed near high pedestal | Endgame target only |

**Ping pong balls** are loaded into a hopper on each side of the field before
the match. Robots collect them during driver control. They are worth more than
rubble and require higher-skill scoring to earn full value.

---

## Scoring

| Action | Points |
|---|---|
| Rubble in Low Zone | 1 pt each |
| Rubble in High Goal | 3 pts each |
| Ping pong ball in Low Zone | 2 pts each |
| Ping pong ball in High Goal | 5 pts each |
| Large ball on High Pedestal | 10 pts |
| Robot parked in Low Zone at end of match | 3 pts |

---

## Match Structure

| Period | Duration |
|---|---|
| Autonomous | 30 seconds |
| Driver Control | 2 minutes |
| **Total** | **2 minutes 30 seconds** |

There is no separate endgame period. Endgame tasks (large ball, parking) are
completed before the final buzzer during driver control.

---

## Starting Positions

- One robot per side (blue and red)
- Robots start within their alliance starting zone
- Starting position is the student's choice within their zone

---

## Endgame Tasks

Both tasks can be completed in the same match. Neither is required.

| Task | How | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Score large ball | Place large ball on High Pedestal before buzzer | 10 pts |
| Park | Robot fully within Low Zone boundary at buzzer | 3 pts |

**Large ball scoring** is the highest single-action score available. It requires
the robot to carry or push the large ball to the pedestal and elevate it.
High risk, high reward. A parked robot earns guaranteed points with no risk.

---

## Differences from Standard Orbit Odyssey

| Rule | Standard | BearBots |
|---|---|---|
| Game elements | Rubble only | Rubble + ping pong balls |
| Element holding limit | 1 at a time | No limit |
| Robot inspection | Size/weight enforced | Identical robots — no inspection |
| Competition format | Alliance-based | 1v1 round-robin |
| Penalties | Standard penalty rules | None |

---

## Notes for Lesson Design

- The ping pong ball hopper is a **player-loaded** element. A human loads balls
  into the hopper; the robot collects from it. This introduces the concept of
  human-robot coordination in a match.
- The **element holding limit is removed** intentionally. Students should focus
  on code and strategy, not hardware constraints.
- The **no-penalty** rule keeps matches friendly and focused on scoring, not
  infractions. Referees (instructor) only track points.
- Point values are designed so **a single large ball + park = 13 pts**, which
  beats **4 rubble in High Goal = 12 pts**. Endgame is always a viable strategy.
