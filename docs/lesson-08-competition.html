Match structure — Orbit Odyssey 1v1

Autonomous — 30 seconds

Hands off the controller. Your code runs. Robot scores rubble, drives to zones, parks — whatever you programmed.

AutoChooser selects your strategy. Set it before you put the robot on the field.

Transition — ~30 seconds

Pick up your controller. Get ready. Check that your robot is where you expect it to be.

Don't touch the robot. Don't touch the controller until teleop is called.

Teleop — 90 seconds

You drive. Complete laps around the field. Carry the amplifier for bonus points. Deposit rubble in zones.

Laps are clockwise. 5 points each. +3 points per lap while carrying the amplifier.

Endgame — last 30 seconds

Return the amplifier to the Earth Pedestal for 10 points. Park in the Low Rubble Zone for 5 more.

Endgame is called at 30 seconds remaining. Plan your return route before the match starts.

Scoring quick reference

Autonomous

Rubble in Low Zone: 1 pt each

Rubble in High Zone: 3 pts each

Park in Low Zone: 5 pts

Teleop

Lap completion: 5 pts

Lap with Amplifier: +3 pts

Endgame

Amplifier returned to Earth: 10 pts

Park in Low Zone: 5 pts

Strategy note — guaranteed points

Park auto (5 pts) + endgame park (5 pts) + amplifier return (10 pts) = 20 points minimum if you execute all three reliably. That's before any laps or rubble. Know your floor before you go for the ceiling.

Pre-match checklist

Run through this before every single match — not just the first one.

  • Robot fits in starting position, all mechanisms within frame perimeter
  • Elevator at bottom, scoop flat
  • AutoChooser set to your chosen strategy
  • Alliance color marker visible and secure
  • Controller connected — test it before the match starts
  • Rubble preloaded if using a scoring auto
The one rule that never changes

Between matches: constants only. No rewriting commands. No new files. No structural changes. If your auto is 10cm short, you change PARK_DISTANCE_METERS in Constants.java — you do not rewrite DriveDistance. This is how real competition works.

You have 30 minutes. Hard stop. Use it well.

Your brain is probably thinking this right now

"I just need to rewrite this one command and it'll work perfectly."

No. If you rewrite a command in 30 minutes without testing it, you will break something that was working. The 30-minute window exists for constants — not rewrites. If something fundamental is broken, adjust your strategy instead. A robot that drives manually and parks every match beats a robot that has a beautiful autonomous that crashes every match.

The tweak workflow

  1. Identify the specific problem from your Lesson 07 field runs
  2. Change one constant in Constants.java
  3. Run in simulator — verify the behavior in AdvantageScope
  4. If sim looks good, confirm with instructor
  5. That's it — don't keep tweaking if it looks right
If your auto is already solid

Close your laptop. Walk the field. Think about your teleop route — where will you pick up the amplifier? What's your path for completing laps efficiently? Which endgame maneuver gets you to the pedestal fastest? Mental prep is real prep.

If something is truly broken

Talk to your instructor. Be specific: "My auto always overshoots by 15cm" is fixable with a constant. "My command never ends" is a structural bug — change your strategy to manual park and compete anyway. One broken session doesn't erase seven lessons of work.

Your brain is probably thinking this right now

"My auto failed in practice. Now what?"

Switch your AutoChooser to your fallback option — or to no auto at all. A match with no autonomous and good teleop still scores points. Stay calm. Drive well. The competition isn't just autonomous — it's three periods and every period is a chance to score.

Between matches

Watch the match in progress

What routes are other drivers taking? How are they handling the amplifier? What's scoring most efficiently?

Note what to adjust

Write it down — don't rely on memory. One constant to change. One driving adjustment. Keep it specific.

One change maximum

If you're going to adjust a constant, change exactly one thing. Run it in sim if time allows. Confirm with instructor.

Scoring — what counts as what

Laps

Robot moves clockwise around the field 360 degrees = one lap = 5 points. Must be complete. Partial laps don't score.

Carrying the amplifier during the lap adds 3 more points.

Parking

Robot partially in the Low Rubble Zone at the end of a period counts as parked. Even partially in counts.

Endgame park: 5 pts. Auto park: 5 pts. Both can score in the same match.

Amplifier return

Amplifier must be on the Earth Pedestal at the end of the match. Dropped next to it does not count.

10 points. Highest single action in the game. Worth going out of your way for.

Teleop strategy — the amplifier math

Each lap is 5 pts. Each lap with the amplifier is 8 pts. If you can complete 3 laps in teleop while carrying the amplifier that's 24 points vs 15 without it — a 9-point swing. But picking up and returning the amplifier costs time. Is the 9-point swing worth the time? That's your call to make.

Penalties

Keep it clean. Penalties give your opponent points — not what you want.

  • Pinning for 5+ seconds — 2 pts to opponent
  • Crossing centerline during auto — 2 pts to opponent
  • Entering opponent's Rubble Zone — 2 pts to opponent
  • Possessing more than 1 rubble — 1 pt per extra rubble
  • Launching or throwing game pieces — red card

In FIRST Robotics, Gracious Professionalism is not a consolation prize. Teams win this award at Worlds. It describes the kind of competitor who helps a struggling opponent fix their robot before a match — because the goal is great robots competing, not just winning.
